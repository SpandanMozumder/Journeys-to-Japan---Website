Hokkaido is Japan's second largest island and comprises the largest and northernmost prefecture, making up its own region. The Tsugaru Strait separates Hokkaidō from Honshu; the two islands are connected by the undersea railway Seikan Tunnel.

The largest city on Hokkaidō is its capital, Sapporo, which is also its only ordinance-designated city. Sakhalin lies about 43 kilometers (26 mi) to the north of Hokkaidō, and to the east and northeast are the Kuril Islands, which are administered by Russia, though the four most southerly are claimed by Japan. Hokkaidō was formerly known as Ezo, Yezo, Yeso, or Yesso.

Its weather is harsh in winter with lots of snowfall, below zero temperatures and frozen seas, while in summer it does not get as hot and humid as in the other parts of the country. With its unspoiled nature, many national parks and rural landscapes, Hokkaido attracts many outdoor lovers, including skiers and snowboarders in the colder seasons and hikers, cyclists and campers from June to September.

Top destinations in Hokkaido

Sapporo

The largest city on the island of Hokkaido.

Otaru

Harbor city near Sapporo with a canal.

Hakodate

Southernmost city on Hokkaido.

Furano

Town with beautiful lavender and flower fields.

Asahikawa

City in central Hokkaido famous for its zoo.

Noboribetsu

Hokkaido's most famous hot spring resort.

Abashiri

Known for its prison and drift ice in winter.

Niseko

Leading snow resort with lots of powder snow.

Kushiro

City in eastern Hokkaido near a large marshland

Jozankei Onsen

Hot spring resort in the mountains outside central Sapporo.

Rusutsu

Attractive ski resort near Niseko.

Tomamu

Large ski resort in central Hokkaido.

Matsumae

The only former castle town on Hokkaido.

Kiroro Ski Resort

Ski resort between Sapporo and Niseko.

National Parks

Akan Mashu National Park

National park with beautiful lakes.

Lake Toya

Site of Mount Usu, an active volcano.

Daisetsuzan

Hokkaido's largest and wildest national park.

Shiretoko

National park on an unspoiled peninsula.

Kushiro Marshlands

Marshlands populated by Japanese cranes.

Lake Shikotsu

Picturesque caldera lake near Sapporo.

Onuma Park

National park 20 kilometers north of Hakodate.

Rishiri and Rebun

Small islands near Hokkaido's northern tip.
